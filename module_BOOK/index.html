2013

  • George RR Martin : A Clash of Kings : Song of Ice and Fire 2: Still a very good series. Will continue reading (Sept13)
  • Reilly, Matthew : Contest : From Elaine. The cover looked like a police procedural, but it turned out to be sci fi almost teenage lit. Very good summer read though (Jun13)
  • Michael Ondaatje : The English Patient : Recommended by Tuula.I wasn't too impressed by this story. As I was reading I could recognise that it was well written but I just felt detatched from the story. Jun2013
  • George RR Martin : Game Of Thrones : From Sanna (Linus's mum). Excellent story. Epic. Lots of characters and intertwining storylines. Twists. Everything needed for me to buy number 2 in the series (May13)
  • Erin Morgenstern : The Night Circus : Brilliant. Enchanting. Magical. A circus which opens at nigtht and is enfused with magic. It is there to be a stage on which 2 magicians can fulfil a challenge which their mentors trained them for. Stunning book. Will read again. Mar13
  • Sansom, CJ : Heartstone : Excellent as always. This time the Queen wants Shardlake to investigate something down near Portsmouth when the Frnech are invading England. He is on the Mary Rose when it sinks. A really good 'historical fiction' note there. Roll on the 6th book! (Feb13)
  • Rankin, Ian : Standing in Another Man's Grave : Rebus is back. Very good as always. I hope the series continues. This was based around dissapearances around the A9 Perth to Inverness via Pitlochry (Jan13)

2012

  • Nesbo, Jo : The Redbreast : First of series. Quite difficult to get into but I didn't have much time to read it so only read a few pages at a time. I'll try the next one anyway.
  • Fry, Stephen : Moab is my Washpot : Excellent autobiography of Stephen Fry's first 20 years. Very well written and heart wrenching.. his life was quite ordinary but at the same time extraordinary.
  • Reynolds, Alistair : Blue Remembered Earth : Excellent. First of Reynolds new trilogy. Geoffrey lives in Africa nad is about to experiment with mind joining with elphants. He is caught up in his space travel, aquatic cities and a discovery about his dead grandmother who lived in a space habitat for all her life.. or so he believes. Looking forward to the next books in this series.... (Oct12)
  • Trotter, William R : The Winter War : Stunning. Well written book which explains, documents and discusses the 1939-1940 Winter War between Russia and Finland. It also clears up the history around Finland's alliance with Germany during the 2nd World War. This book should be re-read every few years as it swells with Finnish spirit and sisu (Sept12)
  • Reynolds, Alistair : Zima Blue : Fantastic collection of short stories with epic SF themes, spanning Reynolds 20 year career. Can't get enough of this author.
  • Pratchett, Terry : The Long Earth : Well.. it was ok, but nothing mind blowing. It was quite childishly written and it's obviously the start of a series. think I'll leave it. Twas all about people 'stepping' to millions of parallel worlds.
  • Cornwell, Bernard : Sharpes Story : Short book by the author of Sharpe about the series. Very good little book. Also a small biography about him being adoped into the 'Peculiar' people. A Christian fundamentalist family... and how they turned him off religion for good :P
  • Reynolds, Alistair : Galactic North : 8 short stories about Felka, Clavain, Worms, All tied together. Well worth the read
  • Sansom, CJ : Revelation : Excellent again. Shardlake is a very 3 dimensional antihero. the story concerns him catching a seriel killer that is using the '7 deadly vials' in the book of Revelation as a template.
  • Channel, AR : The Phantom Patrol : Found this out-of-print book from 1940 on Australian ebay. My Dad has talked about it for years as a favourite. Set in Finnish Lapland at the outbreak of the 1939 Winter War when Petsamo was over run by Russia. Really nice to read as a 'boy's own' scout story as a group of Finns. Lapps and an English teenager disrupt Russian arny attempts by destoyign railway bridges, rescuing prisoners and other 'heroic acts'. The whole book is written with with an 2d anti-Russia pro-stiff-upper-lip and the Finns are characterised as stoic underdogs bathed in ancestral culture.. wonderful stuff! (Apr12)
  • Reynolds, Alistair : Diamond Dogs, Turquoise Days : 2 novellas set in the Revelation Space universe. Diamond Dogs was a very nice compact gothic tale which uses the film 'Cube' as source. Very nicely done. Turquise Days is set on a 'Juggler' world where the sea is full of semi-intelligent organisms and humans swim with them to communicate. A kind of set piece story about a key species in Revelation Space. Very good again. (Apr12)
  • Hosseini, Khaled : A Thousand Splendid Suns : Very well written story focussing on the treatment of women, and the refugee status of many Afghanistans, in Kabul as power is transferred from governments to local warlords to Taliban and back to warlords. Emotionally wrenching and graphically written. I love books of this quality. (Apr12)
  • Thompson, James : Lucifer's Tears : This was a good read. The author sometimes stretches the dialog a bit .. giving thinly veiled lectures on The Winter War, Mannerheim and the Finnish language and culture translation into English is a bit clunky sometimes but maybe that's just because all of this is old ground to me. Quite funny that he describes a birth at Naistenklinnikka exactly where Sampo was born :P (Mar12)
  • Sansom, CJ : Sovereign : (3/5) Very good again. Shardlake, the hunchback lawer, is tasked to go up to York to meet with King Henry VIII's 'Progress' (a massive procession to show the king's power to the northern papist rebels). Pretty action packed with an emphasis on imprisonment for those that oppose the king. Shardlake is tortured in the tower of London .. quie good, gritty description (Mar12)
  • Thompson, James : Snow Angels : 1/3 Finland based crime novels. I enjoyed this very much. Quite a nuts and bolts crime thriller but it was set in Kittila and Levi in the middle of kaamos and so I had the vested interest in reading English language crime thriller set in Finland (which describes the book that is currently slowly developing inside me). I'll read the other 2 in the series. Quick read (Feb12)
  • Reynolds, Alistair : The Prefect : (5/5 Revelation Space books) Stunning part 5 of this series. The "Prefects" are the space security police with the responsibility of looking after a massive planet and the 'Glitterband' ring of 10000 orbiting habitats. A mass murder of one of the habitats triggers an epic space opera including lots of bad guys and a couple of excellent super baddies! Some new technologies (Whiphounds... personal police weapons) and some very touching setpieces and twists. The beheading (and then re-attachment) of the 'head' prefect matriarch figure to save her from the Clockmaker's attached Scarab timebomb device is genius. (Feb12)
  • Cornwell, Bernard : Death of Kings : (Saxon Stories 6/6) Excellent again. Alfred dies and leaves the country at war with the Danes about to launch a full scall attack. Uhtred is getting a bit older but still leads Saxon armies against the Danes. I think there will be another in this series (Feb12)
  • Jenkins, Jerry : Left Behind : This is from the same writing stable as Dan Brown/DaVinciCode. 2D characters who all talk in over-dramatic Americanised statements. I do like the idea of using the book of Revelation as a structure for a whole series of books. This first book describes all the Christians (and kids under 12, babies and unborn children) suddenly vanishing in the rapture when Christ takes them to heaven. A world leader starts to appear and gain power (will become the Antichrist in later books) (Jan12)
  • Fry, Stephen : The Fry Chronicles : Excellent and intimate autobiography covering Stephen Fry's 20th-30th years, and (briefly) his childhood. Some very revealing and funny stuff. Lots of dtails about Cambridge that I loved having lived in Cambridge and Paula having been a student in it's hallowed halls. (Jan12)

2011

  • Reynolds, Alistair : Absolution Gap : (4/5 Revelation Space books) Excellent again. This series is fantastic. Plenty of twists and excellent new technologies and ideas to stretch your mind and imagination. Also action and set pieces are done brilliantly. Apparently this is the best of the 5 books. Let's see when I read the last one! (Dec11 (New Year's Eve!))
  • Clements, Jonathan : Mannerheim: President, Soldier, Spy : Fantastic biography. Well written with attention to detail balanced with pace and plenty of informative maps and photographs of Mannerheim's life. I read this book as Finland was celebrating Independence Day when Mannerheim is remembered for taking the reins to defend Finland's new autonomy against red-russia after the 1917 uprising. He also led the 1940 winter war to stop Russia taking Finnish territory (which is a magnificent piece of Finnish history). I should reread this book every 5 years or so. (Dec11)
  • Hosseini, Khaled : The Kite Runner : Ipad2 book. Fantastically written, emotional, perfect plot arc beautifully and naturally brought to a satisfying conclusion. must read for anyone with a heart. The focus at the end of the book is a small helpless child (Sohrab) who tugs at the heart strings and is a lovely character. I was reading this in hospital when our first baby was born and the mixture of emotions made this book very special. I will read more from this author. (Nov11)
  • Sansom, CJ : Dark Fire : (2/5) Reall nice to read these detective stories set in 1540s. All the historical elements are very well included (for example the idea that all elements are made of earth, water, fire and air) and the action is exciting. The hunchback lawyer is a master stroke of characterization.. very well written.. looking forward to the next books in this series. (11.11.11)
  • Rajaniemi, Hannu : Quantum Thief : Jouni suggested. A little bit too hard core sci-fi for me. It was obviously well written and mostly very good with some excellent ideas, but I had to concentrate too much on following the new technologies and ideas. Too much sci-fi bang and not enough plot and action for me (Nov11)
  • Pratchett, Terry : Snuff : This was quite average for Pratchett. It was still very funny and gave that old 'warm feeling' inside when reading it but there was nothing very new and inventive. A big part of the story relied on the same jokes you would find in Jeeves and Wooster and there was nothing particularly 'Discwoldy' happening until 35% into it (I know this because my iPad deals in % not pages :P)(Nov11)
  • Cornwell, Bernard : The Burning Land : (Saxon Stories 5/6) Excellent again. Historical fiction with plenty of action, battles and blood and guts. The Danes are invading 'England' and Uthred is a Alfred's warlord commanding piecemeal armies to stop them (Oct11)
  • Sansom, CJ : Dissolution : (1/5) Very good story. Very similar to The Name of The Rose. Hunchback Shardlake is sent from Cromwell to investigate a death at a monastary. I'll probably read this whole series (Sept11)
  • Bryson, Bill : At Home : Excellent. Well written. Each chapter is loosely connected to each room in his house. Full of really interesting details from history (Sept11))
  • Reynolds, Alistair : Redemption Ark : (3/5 Revelation Space books) These books are getting better and better. The wolves/inhibitors are about to wipe out humanity culling any intelligent life before it gets too far into space. Various parties are trying to get control of a collection of "Hell class" weapons to mount a defense against the wolves (Aug11)
  • Morris, Donald R : The Washing Of The Spears: The Rise and Fall of the Zulu Nation Under Shaka : Very interesting and easy to read, although I lost interest after Shaka's death (about 150 pages in). The book is a massive endevour.. only for those *really* interested in the exaustive history of the Zulus. (Aug11)
  • Joensuu, Matti : To Steal Her Love : Not as good as Priest of Evil but still readable for a summer pageturner (Jul11)
  • Sansom, CJ : Chasm City : (2/5 Revelation Space books) 1st Ipad2 book. Excellent stand alone story from the 'Revelation Space' series. I finished this and started Redemption Ark immediatly. Reynolds is intelligent, non-patronising and really makes you think about what you are reading.. and these space operas are still action packed and witty. Loving them! (Jul11)
  • Joensuu, Matti : Priest of Evil : Decent crime story. Really refreshing to read a book set in Helsinki, so I could picture all the scenes. Will probably read more of this author (Jun11)
  • Garcia Marquez, Gabriel : Love In The Time Of Cholera : Lovely story about a man's love for a woman from early childhood to old age set in some fictional Carribean location. Very atmospheric and a few truelly beautifully described moments. Lots of various love affairs, noteably the woman who liked to suck different dummy tits and the mad woman from an institution :) (Jun11)
  • Reynolds, Alistair : Revelation Space : (1/5 Revelation Space books) Stunning space opera spanning time, space, civilizations and also packed with action. I've already downloaded Chasm City for as my first Ipad2 book (Jun11)
  • Reynolds, Alistair : Pushing Ice : Excellent, epic sci-fi. An ice-drilling ship (Rockhopper) is sent to follow Janus (a moon of Jupiter) which has suddenly woken up and started to leave the solar system under some unknown power. They get stuck on it and end up millions of years in the future, with various alien races (Fountainheads/Musk Dogs) in a massive 'tube' cage. Will definately read Alistair Reynolds 6 book series. (Mar2011)
  • Faulks, Sebastian : Birdsong : Excellent story of a soldier in WorldWar1 in the trenches at the Somme. The story jumps 1914 - 1978 - 1918 and was very well written. I read this on holiday in Mauritius. One of the 'top 100' that I intend to slowly plough through (Mar11)
  • Cornwell, Bernard : Vagabond : (Grail 2/3) Good fast story, including battles in France and Thomas being questioned by the inquisition about his knowledge as to where the Holy Grail is. Straight onto no3 now... (Feb11)
  • Smith, Wilbur : A Time to Die : (next Courtney after Rage) More actionpacked than usual. Sean Courtney is a seasoned hunter leading a rich guy and his daughter hunting the last old elephant in Africa. Then they get caught up in a guerilla war which involves russian hind helicopters. Good chases and African description. (Feb11)
  • Sansom, CJ : Heretic : (Grail Quest 3/3) Good end to the trilogy. I'm getting a bit jaded just because I've read so much Bernhard Cornwell. I can now recognise his style of writing and even see how his plot arcs are going to resolve themselves. I think I'll give myself a break before reading another. (Feb11)

2010

  • Forester, CS : Hornblower and the Crisis : (unfinished +2 short stories) The last 3 stories of the excellent Hornblower series. Crisis is about Horblower aquiring Napoleon's signature and then planning to use it to send French ships in towards the battle of Trafalgar. Forester died while writing this but his notes give an idea of how it will end. The other 2 short stories were about an Irish rebel trying to send secret documents in a chest back to Ireland and the other story about a 'mad man' claiming to be Napoleon turning up at Hornblower's door, who we later find out to be Napoleon III on his way to France. Hornblower == excellent series. (Dec10)
  • Irving, John : Setting Free the Bears : Very good first book. Strange literary mix of farce, epic storytelling, whimsical descriptive moments and the Nazi invasion of Austria (Dec2010)
  • Larsson, Steig : The Girl Who Kicked the Hornets' Nest : Very good conclusion to the Millenium trilogy and all the ends were tied up, although there was something 'dull' about it. Larsson was very careful to make all the relationships realistic and mature so there was some 'childishness' lacking. Altogether well worth reading. (Dec2010)
  • Kermally, Pete : Waiting for the Punchline : My review on Amazon: Pete Kermally is a modern Tom Sharpe, writing with a real understanding of the London comedy circuit and creating set pieces that would make Wilt proud to have staggered through. A very funny story with some unexpected twists and a few very poigniant moments of deep feeling for the characters. 'Waiting for the Punchline' is as funny as his last set of stories 'Entwined' so hopefully we'll see a whole series of good reads from Kermally. Note: your workmates will start to get irritated after about the 4th or 5th retelling of some of the stand-up oneliners from this book :P
  • Smith, Wilbur : Rage : Excellent, long, epic story about the rise of black opposition to apartheid in South Africa.. Told from the point of view of Afrikaaners, various tribes and factions. Nice that actual historical figures and events are mingled in (eg. Nelson Mandela is a character in this fictional story). (Nov 2010)
  • Larsson, Steig : The Girl Who Played with Fire : Nicely written again. The first book was like a 'locked room' murder mystery on the island. This was more like a police procedural with various action set pieces. I like the writing style (of the English version anyway :) and the non-patronising tone of the narrator. Dan Brown should take note. (Oct 2010)
  • Smith, Roff : Cold Beer and Crocodiles : Very nice diary of a guy cycling anti-clockwise round Australia. Nothing mind blowing.. just a good tale of kangaroos, motels, heat, sheep shearing, desert and a couple of hospital visits (Sept 10)
  • Forester, CS : Hornblower in the West Indies : Action packed from start to finish. First he intercepts a ship intending to free Napoleon from St Helena, lies on his honour that Boney is dead and then finds out that he is in fact dead. Brilliant twist. Disables and catches a fast slave ship by secretly putting a kind of sea anchor to the rudder before she sets off. Pirates catch him in Jamaica and take him to a cliff face. A young millionaire dupes everyone to allow him to sail into Venezuela and pretend to be Hornblower in the rebel war there. A hurricane hits the ship that Hornblower and Barbara are on and the ship nearly sinks in massive swelling seas and hurricane wind... excellent stuff!! (Sept2010)
  • Irving, John : Last Night in Twisted River : Loved this. My first John Irving book but nopt the last. This is a long tale of a cook, his son (a writer) and his grandson who need to flee Twisted River when the writer son kills his father's lover thinking she is a bear :P The cooks long time friend Ketchum is the most heroic anti-hero.. excellent characters and some very funny set pieces. I ordered Irving's first book from the library.. here comes another book series for me! (Aug2010)
  • Fleming, Fergus : Barrow's Boys : Excellently written account of all the expeditions to discover Antarctic, North West Passage, the source of the Nile, Timbuctoo, parts of Australia between 1816 and about 1850. John Barrow was the British Admiralty guy who sent all the explorers to their doom/fame. (Aug2010)
  • Larsson, Steig : The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo : Well plotted. Flawed in parts but worth reading.. maybe DaVinci Code for adults. I've bought the other 2 in the trilogy (Aug10)
  • Forester, CS : Lord Hornblower : Hornblower is asked to deal with a ship of British mutineers near the coast of France. Then he takes control of a French port as the war ends and Napoleon is imprisoned on Elba. Napoleon escapes to Paris and Hornblower is captured in France. He is about to be executed when the Battle of Waterloo turns the tide for him and he is saved at the last gasp. Excellent story again! (Aug10)
  • Rankin, Ian : The Complaints : Very good debut of the new Rankin hero as an internal police investigator. Looking forward to the next ones (Jul10)
  • Forester, CS : The Commodore : Excellent as always. Baltic sea, Finland, St Petersburg, defence of Riga with bomb vessels (Jul10)
  • Forester, CS : Flying Colours : Good solid story. Starts with Hornblower escaping Frensh captives, holding up for a few months in a French sympathiser's home and excaping downriver with Bush and Brown to the safety of a British blockade ship. His court marshal goes well and he is made Comadore. His wife dies when he's in France and he marries Lady Barbara who has taken care of his son after Marie's death (Jun10)
  • Rankin, Ian : A Cool Head : This was a 'Quick Read' (some get people reading drive in the Uk I guess). Nothing particularly special, like the draft of a few Rebus chapters cobbled together for a quick publish. (Jun10)
  • Smith, Wilbur : Power of the Sword : 620 pages so quite epic. Very good story spanning about 20 years from 1930 - 1940 in South Africa. Nelson Mandela and Hitler both turn up and the whole history of Appartheid and prejudice is given a very action packed workover. Excellent stuff although Wilbur Smith's obvious passion for hunting, hairy women and the upper class pursuits (polo, boxing, name dropping all the most expensive makers) still, and I hope will always, irritate the crap out of me. (Jun10)
  • McGregor, Jon : If Nobody Speaks of Remarkable Things : Everybody loved this book. It was shortlisted for prizes. I thought it was beautifully written and very poetic but the 'twist' or climax was like a damp squib. Like Blair Witch Project. I had to google for 30 mins to actually find out what had happened.. had I missed something critical?. I hadn't. I've read plenty books that have been magically and poetically written.. but they had plot too (May2010)
  • Kermode, Mark : Its only a movie : p34 was the first mention of The Excorcist. Anyone who listens to the podcasts will find this quite a surprising display of restraint. Book ws very funny and read just like a long joky conversation about his life. Maybe half of the points you'll know from the radio show.. plenty of interesting little extra stories and anecdotes (Mar10)
  • Pratchett, Terry : Unseen Academicals : Brilliant. Clever. Subtle. Warm humoured. Unpatronising (take note Mr. Brown). Full of nods to the previous Discworld books. I'm already gagging for the next installment (Mar10)
  • Forester, CS : A Ship of the Line : Hornblower is getting better and better. This book was full of action. Even the last 10 pages recounted a battle between Hornblowers ship and 4 French ships (which left him dismasted and surrendered). Bring on the next Hornblower I say! (Mar10)
  • Knecht, C. Bruce : Proving Ground : Highly recommended to anyone. It's a true account of the 1998 Sydney-Hobart(Tasmania) boat race in which 5 men died and ships sank when weather conditions became rediculous. 50 foot waves etc. Scary stuff written in easy documentary style (Feb10)
  • Brown, Dan : The Lost Symbol : Audiobook (unabridged) Even lazier and less inventive than the other 2 bestsellers. Ok for listening to at airports. I know a 6 year old that could produce more imaginative dialogue, action and suspense than this (Jan10)
  • Smith, Wilbur : The Burning Shore : Excellent adventure story. Pure escapism with WW2 France and deepest Africa. I'm hooked on Mr Smith now... embarking on the next in the (long) series.. (Jan10)

2009

  • Bourdain, Anthony : Kitchen Confidential : Excellent account of Bourdain's life. Funny stories about all the jobs he's had over the years (Dec09)
  • Jenkins, Geoffrey : A Bridge of Magpies : Pretty shoddy story of some ex naval captain getting sent to Africa's West Coast for some secret old submarine mission. The author obviously has no experience of relationships between men and women because the romantic core story of this book is laughable. Although this book is marketed at adults it reads like a 12 year old boy wrote it. (Oct09)
  • Horwitz, Tony : Blue Lattitudes : Fantastic way to read history. This is the story of Captain James Cook, told by Tony Horowitz as he retraces Cooks steps round the world with a traveling companion. It made the history really come alive because each (could have been dry/distant) account from Cook's travels are compared to the locations today with discussion and casual jokes/anecdotes. Well worth reading this! (Aug09)
  • Rankin, Ian : Open Doors : Not bad. Basically a Rebus book without Rebus :P Heist set in Edinburgh with a few nice touches.. decent holiday read but no big shakes (Jul09)
  • Dawkins, Richard : Open Doors : Not bad. Basically a Rebus book without Rebus :P Heist set in Edinburgh with a few nice touches.. decent holiday read but no big shakes (Jul09)
  • Forester, CS : The Happy Return (Beat to Quarters) : Again.. excellent story. First Hornblower helps El Supremo (mad Nicaraguan landowner who thinks he's God) to revolt against Spain (because they are allied with France). Furious battle with a ship Natividad which is bigger than Hornblowers. He falls in love with Lady Barbara Wellesley on the long Pacific voyage, but they part on 'formal' terms after he gets guilty about his wife (Jun09)
  • Coelho, Paulo : The Alchemist : Nice fable about a shepherd boy finding the meaning of life in a journey to the Egyptian Pyramids (Jun09)
  • Forester, CS : Hornblower and the Atropos : Excellent mixed adventure. First a well-described canalboat ride to London, then to organise Admiral Nelson's funeral (boat with Nelson's body nearly sinks!), then recover treasure from a sunken British ship in Turkish waters, manages to outwit and escape a Turkish trap and then Following Hornblower's delivery of the treasure, he helps capture a Spanish ship, the Castilla and does such a fine job, his ship (Atropos) attracts the notice of the King of Sicily, who requests and get's it to keep him happy. End of book is in England: both of his children mortally ill with smallpox (Jun09)
  • Cornwell, Bernard : The Sword Song : (Saxon Stories 4/4) Excellent again. Uhtred is in charge of keeping Lundene (900 London) safe in Saxon hands by Alfred (the Great). He just manages to do it after Alfred's daughter is taken by the Danes and Uhtred stages a rescue mission. I see that 5/5 of this series is out in hardback soon. (May09)
  • Cornwell, Bernard : The Lords of the North : (Saxon Stories 3/4) Uhtred takes Northumbria back and kills his childhood enemies. For 2 years he is a slave on a ship sailing between Denmark and Britain. Good actaion again and very bloody battles. Christianity is coming to Britain and Alfred is embracing it (May09)
  • Cornwell, Bernard : The Pale Horseman : (Saxon Stories 2/4) Uhtred is now 20 years old and in the prime of his fighting life. Wessex (England) is almost completely destroyed by invading Danes but Alfred survives in a marsh and calls all Saxons together for a last attempt battle against the Danes at the Battle of Ethandun. Uthred and the Saxons then (just) kick the Danes asses (Apr09)
  • Bryson, Bill : A Walk in the Woods : The Apalachian Trail through the eastern US. Very good light read with plenty of laughs. Would be a good holiday book (Mar09)
  • Obama, Barrack : Dreams from My Father : The story of Obama and his search to find out about his family. Parents, grandparents, tribe and ancestral heritage. I suppose the best outcome of reading this autobiography is that it shows that he's an intelligent, thoughtful, knowledgable man who has experienced a lot that will help him make decisions as president. Well worth the read (and no real politics :O) (Mar09)
  • Koontz, Dean : Odd Hours : Odd is in a place called Magic Beach thwarting some attempt to deliver nukes somewhere. It's all foggy and there are coyotes around. This was a dull and unplotted episode in an otherwise excellent series (Mar09)
  • Bauby, Jean-Dominique : The Diving Bell and the Butterfly : Maybe I'm hardening up in my old age but I read this book in a day and, although I do get that he transcribed it all with an eyelid and the imagery is all spot on for his unique situation, it didn't really move me much. There seemed too much romanticism about France which generally leaves me cold (Feb09)
  • Koontz, Dean : Brother Odd : Odd is now living in a monastery. Excellent story with apparitions monsters weird and wonderfullality. Roll on Odd Hours... (Feb09)
  • Slash : Slash (Autobiography) : (Autobiography) How this guy is still breathing is a mystery to me. By the age of 20 he'd pickled himself with drugs&drink. Very good read.. it ends with Velvet Revolver's Libertad album in 2007 (Feb09)

2008

  • Bryson, Bill : A Short History of Nearly Everything : Fantastic popular science book. Very easy and fun to read and covers all the 'BIG' questions about life on earth/how we got here/the Earth itself/chemistry/physics/fossils/darwin.. everything! (Dec08)
  • Kermally, Pete : Entwined : Excellent group of long&short stories about linked characters. A bit like Irvine Welsh meets Tom Sharpe (Nov08)
  • Forester, CS : Hornblower and the Hotspur : Starting to get to like Hornblower. He is a quietly calculating man and the books are written with his emotions and goals in mind. Some very nice descriptions of seafaring. I'll keep reading this series...(Sept08)
  • Forester, CS : Lieutenant Hornblower : (2/???)(Sept08)
  • Forester, CS : Mr Midshipman Hornblower : (1/???)(Sept08)
  • Mandela, Nelson : A Long Walk to Freedom: The Autobiography of Nelson Mandela : Excellently written with plenty of smiles. Very sad moments throughout but thouroughly enjoyable. I'd like to read some Afrikaaner/white version of event to find out how the oppressors justified apartheid (Aug08)
  • Cornwell, Bernard : The Last Kingdom : (Saxon Stories 1/4) Fantastic story. 9th Century Britain. Viking (Dane) invasion. Uhtred grows up with Danes and then returns to fight as a Saxon. Still to read 2-4 (Jun08)
  • Dawkins, Richard : The God Delusion : Well structured and very interesting reasoning for why intelligent people can say they are athiests. Discussion of the existence of God, the bible, religion, evolution, morality and lots more. I would say that I agree with most of what Dawkins states here as it is mostly logical, common sense (May08)
  • Cornwell, Bernard : Sharpe's Devil : Last Sharpe book *sniff*. Captain-General Vivar is missing in Chile and his wife pays Sharpe to find him. Patrick Harper (now fat) goes to. Imprisoned Napoleon on St Helena gives Sharpe a picture to take to Chile. Admiral Cochrane, a Scottish Lord turned rebel seaman. Climactic battle at Valdivia against nasty General Bautista (mar08) #21
  • Cornwell, Bernard : Sharpe's Waterloo : There is so mush of the battle here that all other plot is pushed aside.. all good description of a horrible event. (Feb 08)
  • Rankin, Ian : Exit Music : The last Rebus book. Very good end to the series with a nice 'cliffhanger' ending.. Cafferty being recussed by Rebus.. and we never know whether he dies or not. The story centres around the murder of a Russian poet visiting Edinburgh. (Jan07)
